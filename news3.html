Bitcoin Price Tops $4,800, Reaching New High!

Posted on 1st Septemer 2017

Bitcoin prices topped $4,800 today, reaching a new high.

The digital currency's price climbed to as much as $4,802.40 during the session, according to CoinDesk's Bitcoin Price Index (BPI).

This price represented one of many fresh highs that Bitcoin has reached this year.

Year-to-date, Bitcoin's price has surged nearly 400%, additional BPI figures show.

This year, the cryptocurrency has surpassed key price levels of $2,000, $3,000 and $4,000. Now, Bitcoin prices are nearing $5,000, the next major price target.

Overcoming Hard Fork Concerns

Bitcoin prices have continued to rise even though the cryptocurrency could potentially split in two come November when the Bitcoin network undergoes a hard fork.

Should the network divide into two separate blockchains, that will mean there are three versions of Bitcoin: the two that survive after the fork and Bitcoin Cash, which came into existence August 1.

The fact that Bitcoin prices keep climbing in spite of this uncertainty shows the robust enthusiasm surrounding the digital currency.

Growing Investor Interest

Another development that should be noted is the growing interest that the broader cryptocurrency space has been drawing from investors.

As more market participants take notice of these digital assets, the total market capitalization of all cryptocurrencies has snowballed, surging from roughly $17.7 billion at the start of the year to a fresh, all-time high of $172.9 billion today, according to CoinMarketCap.

Widespread Rally

As funds continue to flow into the space, several cryptocurrencies have benefited, and alternative protocol assets like Litecoin and Monero have surged to fresh, record highs.

Interestingly enough, many of these so-called altcoins have enjoyed far stronger price rallies than Bitcoin.

Litecoin, for example, has climbed more than 1,500% this year, its price rising from $4.33 to more than $70, CoinMarketCap figures show.

Bubble Concerns

As cryptocurrencies continue their unbridled rally, some market observers have warned that a bubble may be forming.

However, it is important to keep in mind that while these digital currencies have surged in value this year, not everyone believes their prices are inflated.

In the grand scheme of things, market observers may only find whether there is a bubble in hindsight.
